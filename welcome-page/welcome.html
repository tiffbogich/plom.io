It's time to change the way we do model-based science.

The original goals of scientific journals were to provide fast delivery of science, reproducible results, and communities of natural scientists. Modeling papers present a new challenge to these original goals, given the lack of source code, build and runtime issues, the time consuming and error-prone nature of reimplementation, and a culture of reinventing, rather than recycing, of models.

PLoM.io provides a new ecosystem for model-based science.

PLoM.io takes advantage of the fully dematerialized nature of model-based sciences and uses a 100% web approach. PLoM.io leverages the web to boost productivity and the diffusion of science. The web approach of PLoM.io includes an automated peer review process, providing the reproducibility and scalability lacking in the current ecosystem.

PLoM.io makes model-based science more efficient.

With the click of a button, PLoM.io allows you to start at the cutting edge of scientific activity. By 'forking' an existing model in the library, and leveraging the fully tested statistical library of inference methods, PLoM minimises the need to write code and build models from scratch.

PLoM.io makes model-based science social.

With the click of a button, PLoM.io allows you to 'commit' your work to the library of models, receiving an automated review report and the chance to request feedback from your peers. PLoM.io fosters an open access community, bringing model-based science up to speed with the latest web technology and back to the original tenants of scientific publishing.
